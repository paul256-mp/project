Site Plan Document

Site Name

Pearl of Africa Explorer

This name was chosen to highlight Uganda's nickname as the "Pearl of Africa" while clearly communicating the website's purpose as a tourism guide. It evokes the country's exceptional natural beauty and diverse wildlife that Winston Churchill famously praised.

Domain availability: pearlagricaexplorer.com or visituganda.travel

Site Purpose

The Pearl of Africa Explorer serves as Uganda's premier online tourism portal, showcasing the country's breathtaking landscapes, diverse wildlife, and rich cultural heritage. The site will provide:

  • Comprehensive information on national parks and wildlife reserves
  • Detailed guides to gorilla and chimpanzee trekking experiences
  • Cultural tourism opportunities and heritage site information
  • Travel planning resources including accommodation, transportation, and seasonal advice
  • Photography galleries showcasing Uganda's natural beauty
  • Booking services for tours and safari experiences

Scenarios

These questions represent what potential visitors to Uganda might ask when planning their trip:

Scenario 1: Wildlife Enthusiast

"I want to see mountain gorillas in their natural habitat. Which national park offers the best gorilla trekking experience, what permits are required, and what is the best time of year to visit?"

Scenario 2: Adventure Traveler

"I'm interested in combining wildlife viewing with adventure activities. Where can I find information about white-water rafting on the Nile, hiking Rwenzori Mountains, and visiting multiple national parks in one trip?"

Scenario 3: Cultural Tourist

"Beyond wildlife, I'd like to experience Ugandan culture. What traditional kingdoms, cultural sites, and local community experiences can I include in my itinerary?"

Color Schema

The selected color palette is inspired by Uganda's national colors and natural landscapes, creating a visually appealing and culturally relevant design.

Uganda Green
#006400

Primary Color (Uganda Green): Used for headings, navigation elements, and primary buttons. This deep green represents Uganda's lush forests and national parks.

Uganda Yellow
#FFD700

Secondary Color (Uganda Yellow): Used for highlights, call-to-action buttons, and accents. This golden yellow represents sunshine and the country's vibrant energy.

Earth Brown
#8B4513

Accent Color (Earth Brown): Used for backgrounds, borders, and secondary elements. This rich brown represents Uganda's fertile soil and traditional crafts.

Dark Slate
#2F4F4F

Text Color (Dark Slate): Used for body text and standard content. Provides excellent readability while maintaining a natural feel.

Beige
#F5F5DC

Background Color (Beige): Used for page background and content containers. Creates a warm, natural appearance reminiscent of safari landscapes.

Typography

The selected fonts combine elegance with readability, reflecting both the sophistication of modern tourism and the natural beauty of Uganda.

Merriweather

Usage: Headings (H1, H2, H3), logo, and special highlights.

Merriweather provides an elegant, sophisticated look that works well for headings and important text elements, conveying the premium nature of Uganda's tourism offerings.

Sample: The Pearl of Africa Awaits Your Discovery

Open Sans

Usage: Body text, navigation items, buttons, and general content.

Open Sans is highly readable and works well for longer text passages, ensuring comfortable reading experience across all devices.

Sample: Uganda is home to half of the world's remaining mountain gorillas, making it one of the most unique wildlife destinations on the planet.
